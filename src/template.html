About me

It all started a couple of years back when, after friends of mine kept on telling me that I'd make a great developer, and I decided to teach myself how to code.

I'm the kind of person who's naturally curious. In coding, this means I've invested in all sorts of things. On the more conventional end, I've done my time in Python and JavaScript (React/Node), and on the more niche I've been fascinated and dabbled in whatever is grabbing my attention, including but not limited to machine learning (four courses on deeplearning.ai, primarily TensorFlow), privacy tools (QubesOS, Tor), Haskell, DevOps, and Linux.

I discovered blockchain soon into my journey, and I've remained a huge fan of Ethereum ever since. I've contributed to a number of libraries (web3.py, Trinity, Vyper), attended Devcon IV, and am a member of the Ethereum Cat Herders, a volunteer group dedicated to decentralized product management and coordination.
